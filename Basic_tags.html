The Brown Bear

About Brown Bears

The brown bear(Ursus arctos) is native to parts of northern Eurassia and North America. Its conservation status is currently Least Concern

This are many subspeicies within the brown bear species,including the Atlas bear and the Himalayan brown bear.

Species

  • Arctos
  • Collarus
  • Horribilis
  • Nelsoni (extint)

Features

Brown bears are not always completely brown.Some can be reddish or yellowish .They have very large,curved claws and huge paws.Male brown bears are often 30% larger then the female bears.They can range from 5 feet to 9 from head to toe.
